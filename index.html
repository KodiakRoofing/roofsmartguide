Press Release: Kodiak Roofing Debunks the Myth of the 25-Year Workmanship Warranty

June 25, 2025

SACRAMENTO, CA — In an industry where longer warranties often masquerade as better protection, Kodiak Roofing, a trusted leader in roofing excellence, is setting the record straight: when it comes to workmanship warranties, 10 years of real accountability is often more valuable than 25 years of marketing hype.

“As more roofing companies advertise 25-year workmanship warranties, we’re seeing an alarming trend — customers are being misled,” said Chris Madden, VP of Operations at Kodiak Roofing. “These extended warranties sound impressive but are rarely backed by anything real or enforceable.”

Understanding the Difference

Unlike material warranties, which are typically provided by manufacturers, workmanship warranties are promises made by the installer. A 10-year workmanship warranty from a reputable company like Kodiak Roofing means:

  • Clear coverage of installation-related defects
  • Immediate support if something goes wrong
  • A team that’s still around to honor the warranty

By contrast, many 25-year workmanship warranties offered by non-certified contractors:

  • Include fine print that voids the warranty under common conditions
  • Are non-transferable or prorated into uselessness
  • Are offered by businesses that statistically won’t exist in 10 years

Expert Consensus

Most roofing industry experts agree: installation errors will show up within the first 2–5 years, if they happen at all. A 10-year workmanship warranty is more than sufficient to detect and fix any issues that result from the initial installation.

“Offering a 25-year warranty doesn’t make a contractor better — it just makes their sales pitch longer,” said Madden. “Our commitment is to transparency, quality, and service. That’s why our 10-year workmanship warranty is honest, clear, and dependable.”
